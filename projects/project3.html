Via Alpina Trek Itinerary

Day 1 – Arrive in Vaduz | Liechtenstein Warm-Up Walk

Arrive by train in Sargans or Buchs, Switzerland, then take a bus to Vaduz, the capital city of the mountainside Principality of Liechtenstein. The tour will start at 2:00 pm with a short warm-up walk up to Vaduz Castle, the palace and residence of Liechtenstein’s ruling family. There is no border control between Switzerland and Liechtenstein, but you'll want to stop at the tourism office to have your passport ceremonially stamped. Then, take a photo of yourself standing on the official starting point of the Via Alpina in the pedestrian center of cosmopolitan Vaduz. In the evening, enjoy an orientation and welcome dinner. Overnight in Vaduz.

Ascent: 400 feet | Descent: 400 feet | Distance: 2 miles (may vary)

Day 2 – Vaduz to Weisstannen

A morning transfer from Vaduz, Liechtenstein to the Sargans castle in Sargans, Switzerland positions us to get our first Via Alpina route stamp. We then make our way to Mels for a quick ascent to high alpine pastures, where our route follows an undulating course through woods and meadows with relaxing views of the Weisstannental and its many waterfalls. We pass a dozen or more streams and rivulets cascading down the hillside. The Weisstannental is the canton of St. Gallen’s longest valley, and we arrive at our hotel along a mountain stream in the peaceful village of Weisstannen. Overnight in Weisstannen.

Ascent: 2500 feet | Descent: 1000 feet | Distance: 8 miles (may vary)

Day 3 – Weisstannen to Elm

As we continue ascending the Weisstannental, the route narrows and becomes more rugged before passing above a giant chasm leading to the high meadows at Fooalp. Crossing our first pass, the Foopass (pronounced FOE-pass), takes us to an elevation of 7300 feet and gives you a taste for the more challenging alpine passes ahead. The descent to Elm offers stunning scenery with many large waterfalls streaking the hillsides. Elm is located in the heart of the Sardona Tectonic Arena, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and is known for its specialty foods including fine chocolates and pastries. Overnight in a spa hotel in the heart of Elm.

Ascent: 3000 feet | Descent: 4300 feet | Distance: 10 miles (may vary)

Day 4 – Elm to Braunwald

From Elm, enjoy views of the Martinsloch, a giant hole in the Alps that reveals the geological forces that shaped the area. A morning bus will speed you from Elm to a ski hut at Obererbs. The ascent is steep as you reach the narrow saddle marking the Richetlipass at 7418 feet. The descent from the pass is unusually steep down to Unterstafel, so be surefooted and consider using trekking poles as you take your time. Arriving in the valley center of Linthal, you’ll find a funicular ready to whisk you to the car-free alpine resort of Braunwald, set on a grassy slope with mesmerizing mountain views, including the glaciated Tödi massif, the highest mountain in the Glarner Alps. Spend the evening basking in the mountain views and starlit sky. Overnight in Braunwald.

Ascent: 2300 feet | Descent: 5700 feet | Distance: 10 miles (may vary)

Day 5 – Braunwald to Klausenpass

Today’s route takes you on a high path above the valley leading to Urnerboden, the village at the head of Switzerland’s largest Alp. The mountain views are astounding and reminiscent of the Dolomites. You’ll have a chance to stock up on cheese from a large dairy in Urnerboden before taking a bus transfer or hiking the rest of the way to Klausenpass. The historic hotel at the top of Klausenpass, established in 1902 and newly renovated in 2021, offers unmatched views and traditional hospitality. Overnight in Klausenpass.

Ascent: 1500-3300 feet | Descent: 1450-1750 feet | Distance: 8-11.5 miles (may vary)

Day 6 – Klausenpass to Altdorf

The Klausenpass is a historic route used for centuries as a trade route connecting hamlets and farms on both sides of the pass. As you descend the valley, several impressive waterfalls catch your eye – notably the Stäuber waterfall above the hamlet of Äsch, possibly the most scenic undiscovered waterfall in Switzerland. Continue into Altdorf via the old town of Bürglen, home of William Tell, where the 16th-century chapel and the Tell Museum bring history to life. In Altdorf, admire the monument of William Tell with his crossbow and pierced apple. Overnight in Altdorf.

Ascent: 140-1090 feet | Descent: 2860-4000 feet | Distance: 4.5-7.5 miles (may vary)

Day 7 – Altdorf to Engelberg

Start the day by crossing the Reuss River, descending from the historic Gotthard Pass. A small cable car from the mountainside village of Attinghausen will take you to Brüsti, just above treeline. The route to the Surenenpass ascends beneath towering cliffs with the peaks of the Brunnistock and Blackenstock overhead. A large permanent snowfield provides an efficient ascent to the pass, where views of Mt. Titlis dominate the panorama. Overnight in Engelberg.

Ascent: 3050-3600 feet | Descent: 2600-4470 feet | Distance: 10-12 miles (may vary)

Day 8 – Engelberg | Mt. Titlis Excursion

With plenty to do in the Engelberg mountain resort, today is far from a rest day. Ascend Mt. Titlis – one of the giants of the Uri Alps – via the world’s first rotating cable car. At the summit, explore a glacier cave and enjoy a cliff walk. Later, visit the 12th-century monastery housing an exquisite baroque Abbey. You will still have time to explore Engelberg’s charming pedestrian town center. Overnight in Engelberg. Guests have the option to enjoy lunch and dinner on their own.

Day 9 – Engelberg to Engstlenalp

Ascend above Engelberg into an expanse of high alpine lakes and grassy meadows. Pass by Trübsee and cross the Jochpass to reach Engstlensee, a realm where marmots roam and wildflowers flourish. Choose between a strenuous stretch or a leisurely pace with the help of cable cars. Arrive at the traditional Alpine hamlet of Engstlenalp, and settle in at the Hotel Engstlenalp, a mountain hotel dating back to the 1860s.

Note: You will spend this evening in a Berghotel. Private rooms are provided, with shared bathrooms. There is no elevator, so you may need to carry your luggage up several flights of stairs.

Ascent: 60-4150 feet | Descent: 1270-1500 feet | Distance: 2.5-8 miles (may vary)

Day 10 – Engstlenalp to Meiringen

Departing Engstlenalp, you will soon reach the charming Alpine hamlet of Tannalp, known for its tiny church and friendly goats. Skirt the Tannensee to ascend the Erzegg/Planplatten Ridge – a highlight of the Via Alpina and one of the great ridge walks in the Alps. As the highest peaks and glaciers of the Bernese Alps come into view, with the Wetterhorn dominating the landscape, descend along the ridge into the popular town of Meiringen, famed as the birthplace of meringues. Overnight in Meiringen.

Ascent: 2200 feet | Descent: 980-3780 feet | Distance: 7-11 miles (may vary)

Day 11 – Meiringen to Grindelwald

Meiringen, located at the base of four historic passes along the Aare River, has long attracted international travelers, including Sir Arthur Conan Doyle. Begin by ascending the falls by funicular, then continue up the picturesque Rosenlaui Valley. Traverse a thundering gorge draining the Rosenlaui Glacier and cross the final pass of our trek, the famed Grosse Scheidegg. Arrive in Grindelwald, where you will check into a hotel adorned with flower-filled balconies and views of the Eiger’s North Face. Overnight in Grindelwald.

Ascent: 2200-4950 feet | Descent: 500-3500 feet | Distance: 7.5-14.5 miles (may vary)

Day 12 – Depart from Grindelwald

After breakfast, plan your departure or onward travel. Concluding the tour in Grindelwald places you in an ideal location to extend your stay in this popular mountain resort. Consider a classic mountain railway excursion through the Eiger to the Jungfraujoch, the highest railway station in Europe, overlooking the Great Aletsch Glacier. Alternatively, explore other hikes in the Jungfrau Region. Please note that the train ride to Zürich airport takes approximately 3.5 hours, with the first train departing Grindelwald at 5:17 am. Plan your departure flights accordingly. We can recommend hotels in Zürich if needed.
